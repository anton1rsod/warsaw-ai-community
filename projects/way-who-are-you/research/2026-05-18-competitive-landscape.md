## Goal

Catalog the competitive landscape for W.A.Y. and identify where each competitor is strong / weak / non-overlapping. Output: a comparison matrix per category + a SWOT analysis specifically for W.A.Y.'s positioning against the field.

## Why this matters

- **Sharpens §1 Problem framing** — when we know exactly who is solving adjacent problems badly, the W.A.Y. wedge gets clearer.
- **Calibrates §9 risk severity** — if 5+ well-funded competitors are pushing "AI twin" or "persona layer" framing in 2026, the "Anthropic ships persona" risk is no longer the biggest threat; it's market crowding.
- **Drives differentiation copy** — landing page, founder pitch, HN launch post all need to position against actual products users have heard of.
- **Identifies acquisition opportunities** — failed competitors leave users looking for alternatives.

## Method

1. **Web search** for current (May 2026 onward) products in each category below.
2. **Hands-on exploration** of top 2-3 competitors in each category — free tier trial; install and use; note onboarding flow, UX patterns, output quality.
3. **Read founder interviews / launch posts / Twitter / Hacker News** to understand stated positioning vs. actual user reaction.
4. **Check ProductHunt, Hacker News, IndieHackers, LinkedIn launches** for recent (last 90 days) entrants in adjacent categories.
5. **Track pricing changes** for the major platforms (Claude / ChatGPT / Notion / etc.) — these are exogenous threats to all "persona layer" startups.

## Competitor categories

1. **Persona / AI-twin products** — direct positioning competitors
2. **Productivity AI assistants / agents** — adjacent threats; not personas but solve similar pain
3. **Custom GPT / Claude Project tooling** — DIY substitutes from the major platforms themselves
4. **Brand voice / writing tools** — voice-specific competitors (Lavender, Jasper, Copy.ai)
5. **Skill marketplaces** — distribution-channel competitors (mostly tracked in [platform compatibility matrix](2026-05-18-platform-compatibility-matrix.md))
6. **Founder-specific AI tools** — vertical competitors (emerging category in 2026)
7. **Local LLM / on-device AI tools** — opportunity/threat from Apple MLX direction

## SWOT (W.A.Y. specifically — filled after competitor matrix completes)

### Strengths

- Deep, opinionated questionnaire (inherited design discipline from `persona-builder`)
- Cross-platform portability (open Skills standard, multi-vendor adoption)
- Privacy-first architecture (client-side persona, no harvesting)
- Solo-founder iteration speed (Claude Code as build accelerator)
- Anton's direct founder-network access (acquisition channel)
- Founder-specific positioning (not generic)
- "Persona layer for your AI stack" tagline (L22) — category-creating

### Weaknesses

- Solo founder; single point of failure
- No external funding; bootstrap pace
- Brand new; zero reputation outside Anton's network
- No marketplace producer ecosystem yet
- Dependent on open Skills standard health
- Geography (Poland / EU) may slow US founder acquisition

### Opportunities

- Multi-axis questionnaire variants (BACKLOG approved direction) — deep moat once shipped
- Cross-platform install wizards — each new platform = expanded TAM
- Platform partnership badges (BACKLOG) — credibility multipliers
- Apple MLX + on-device LLM trend — strengthens "your laptop" positioning
- Track 2 consulting differentiator — humans + product combined (counter to "AI ships native feature" threat)
- Underserved European founder cohort (US-centric competitors miss)

### Threats

- Anthropic / OpenAI ships native persona feature (mitigated by W.A.Y.'s expert-authored guided service + Track 2 fallback per Anton 2026-05-18)
- Claude Pro pricing changes (HIGH risk per §9; mitigated by open standard + MLX trend)
- Skill marketplace saturation drowning "persona layer" message
- Founder-AI category becomes commoditized faster than W.A.Y. can ship
- Regulatory pushback on AI-personalization tools (EU AI Act enforcement?)
